About Me

I’m Anurag Anirudha Kelkar, a software developer who fell in love with code the first time I made a computer print “Hello, World!” and realized it actually listened to me. Since then, I’ve been fascinated by how a few lines of code can build entire applications, optimize complex systems, and sometimes, break everything in the process (but hey, that’s what debugging is for).

At the University of Florida, where I’m pursuing my Master’s in Computer Science (GPA 3.89—because perfection is a moving target), I’ve dived deep into full-stack engineering, system design, and data-driven applications. As a Graduate Student Assistant (Web Developer), I built Python/Flask APIs for OCR processing and fine-tuned React frontends to make them smoother than my morning coffee. My internships at Securonix India gave me hands-on experience with the agile software development lifecycle, teaching me the importance of structured development, modular design, and why last-minute bug fixes before a release are always a terrible idea.

I work primarily with Python, Go, JavaScript, and TypeScript, and I’ve built projects that have challenged me to think critically about system architecture and scalability. One of my favorites is a workout tracker app using React, Go, and PostgreSQL—because if I can track my code’s performance. Another project I enjoyed was a news aggregator that doesn’t just scrape headlines but intelligently clusters articles using NLP, making it easier to follow evolving stories. Then there’s the library management system, where I used Red-Black Trees to optimize search and retrieval, finally giving this classic data structure some real-world justice. Each project has pushed me to refine my problem-solving skills, balance efficiency with usability, and, occasionally, fight with API rate limits at 2 AM.

Beyond coding, I have an obsession with astronomy (I still hold out hope for intergalactic travel), enjoy playing chess (a game where I overthink every move), and love cooking (my experiments in the kitchen are almost as unpredictable as my debugging sessions). I also find peace in hiking, getting lost in music, and contemplating the mysteries of the universe—like why Wi-Fi always stops working when you need it most.

At my core, I love to build, learn, and collaborate. I thrive in environments that challenge me to think differently, push boundaries, and write code that (hopefully) doesn’t crash. Whether it’s software engineering or system design, I’m always on the lookout for opportunities to work on impactful projects, contribute to innovative teams, and make technology just a little more fun.
